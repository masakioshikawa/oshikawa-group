Research Interests

Our research focuses on fundamental problems in theoretical condensed matter physics, particularly in quantum many-body systems. We employ various theoretical and computational methods to understand the physics of strongly correlated systems.

Quantum Many-Body Systems

We investigate strongly correlated electron systems and quantum phase transitions using analytical and numerical methods. Our work includes:

  • Quantum phase transitions in low-dimensional systems
  • Strongly correlated electron systems
  • Frustrated quantum magnets
  • Quantum entanglement in many-body systems

Topological Phases of Matter

Study of topological order, topological insulators, and topological quantum computation:

  • Topological phases and topological order
  • Symmetry-protected topological phases
  • Topological insulators and superconductors
  • Applications to quantum computation

Low-Dimensional Quantum Systems

Analysis of one-dimensional and two-dimensional quantum systems:

  • One-dimensional quantum systems and Luttinger liquids
  • Two-dimensional quantum spin systems
  • Quantum Hall effect and edge states
  • Boundary effects in quantum systems

Conformal Field Theory

Applications of conformal field theory to condensed matter physics:

  • Critical phenomena and universality
  • Conformal bootstrap methods
  • Finite-size scaling and boundary conformal field theory
  • Entanglement entropy in critical systems

Theoretical Methods

We employ a wide range of theoretical tools including:

  • Quantum field theory methods
  • Renormalization group techniques
  • Exact solutions and integrability
  • Numerical simulations (DMRG, exact diagonalization)
  • Tensor network methods
